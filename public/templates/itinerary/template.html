This itinerary balances city time in Barcelona with a slower stretch along the Costa Brava. Each day is structured enough to follow without feeling over-planned — built from real experience, not a generic template.

Day 1

Arrive in Barcelona

Morning

Land & check in

Arrive BCN, taxi or metro to El Born. Drop bags, walk the neighborhood before committing to a full schedule.

Afternoon

Gothic Quarter walk

Cathedral, Plaça Reial, and a slow loop through the old city. Stop for coffee at a terrace — this is a reset day, not a checklist day.

Evening

Dinner in El Born

Reserve a table in El Born or Gràcia. Keep it simple on night one: tapas, wine, early night if you're jet-lagged.

Tip: Buy a T-Casual metro pass if you'll ride transit more than twice today.

Day 2

Gaudí & Eixample

Morning

Sagrada Família

Pre-book a timed entry. Allow 90 minutes inside. The light through the stained glass is best late morning.

Afternoon

Passeig de Gràcia

Walk the block of modernist facades — Casa Batlló and La Pedrera are worth seeing even from the street if you skip the interiors.

Evening

Sunset at Bunkers del Carmel

Taxi up for the view, walk back down through Gràcia for dinner. Arrive 45 minutes before sunset for a good spot.

Before You Go

Book ahead

  • Sagrada Família — timed entry required
  • Any Michelin-adjacent dinner reservations
  • Train to Girona if traveling on a weekend

Getting around

  • T-Casual pass for metro and buses
  • Taxi or FGC for early airport departures
  • Walk El Born and Gothic Quarter — driving is pointless

What to pack

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light layer for coastal evenings
  • EU plug adapter

Worth knowing

  • Lunch is typically 1:30–3:30 PM
  • Dinner starts late — 8:30 PM is early
  • Sunday mornings many shops stay closed
